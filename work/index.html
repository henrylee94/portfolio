Case 01 · featured

Transaction Backbone

Rebuilt a legacy transaction flow into a durable workflow service with safe retries and self-service integration.

millions/day 100+ SDK releases 30–60 min → ~2 min onboarding 200+ types · one base workflow

02roads not taken

Roads not taken

Every architecture I run replaced one I personally operated. Here's what lost, and why.

Cron and scan, versus durable workflows

The old platform ran 22+ cron jobs, one of them re-scanning for stuck approvals every single minute. I maintained that for six years — so when durable workflows arrived, I didn't need a blog post to explain why retries belong in the engine, not in a scanner. What would change my mind: a workload where every task is truly fire-and-forget.

Tune the database harder, versus add a cache

Redis was not the first answer — months of index work, aggregation rewrites and dead-index deletion came first. The cache earned its place only after the database was already honest. What would change my mind: nothing; the order itself is the principle.

The benchmark that lost, then won

An analytics engine failed our JSON-path queries — 3 to 15 times slower. Instead of accepting the verdict, we flattened the schema and removed the weakness, and the verdict changed. Benchmarks are inputs to architecture, not judges of it.

Local models, versus a tiered router

At home I wanted local models for everything — free, private, mine. CPU-only inference lost to multi-round tool calling by an order of magnitude, so a tiered router took its place. Losing cleanly beats winning on paper.
